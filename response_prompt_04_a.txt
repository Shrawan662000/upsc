### Question 1: 
Which of the following statements regarding the Hoolock Gibbon is/are correct?  
1. The Eastern Hoolock Gibbon is classified as Endangered by the IUCN.  
2. The largest population of Hoolock Gibbons is found in the Hollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary in Assam.  
3. Both the Eastern and Western Hoolock Gibbons are listed in Schedule I of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.  

Options:  
A) 1 and 2 only  
B) 2 and 3 only  
C) 1 and 3 only  
D) 1, 2, and 3  

**Answer:** B) 2 and 3 only  
**Explanation:** The Eastern Hoolock Gibbon is classified as Vulnerable, not Endangered. The largest population is indeed found in the Hollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary, and both species are listed in Schedule I of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.
